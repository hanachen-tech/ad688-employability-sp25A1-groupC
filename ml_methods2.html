Model Performance

To model salary based on location, job title, and skills, we applied Multiple Linear Regression (MLR) using PySpark’s ML pipeline. The analysis was conducted on 30,808 job postings with valid salary data.

The model achieved an R² of 0.378 and Adjusted R² of 0.375, explaining approximately 38% of salary variation. The minimal gap between R² and Adjusted R² confirms that the additional variables contribute meaningfully rather than inflating model performance artificially. The base salary (intercept) is estimated at $79,072, representing the average compensation for a baseline profile in the dataset.

In addition to the three core predictors (location, job title, and skills), we incorporated three supplementary variables to improve model accuracy: employment type (full-time vs. part-time), remote work type, and minimum years of experience. These variables were selected based on data availability — both employment type and remote type had 100% non-null rates, while minimum years of experience had 76.9% coverage and was imputed using the median value.

Salary Impact by Feature

The coefficient table reveals how each feature influences salary relative to the baseline:

Job Title has the strongest directional impact. Data Engineer Analytics roles are associated with the highest salary premium (+$126,432), followed by Oracle HCM Cloud Manager (+$54,722) and Business Intelligence Sr. Analyst (+$24,379). In contrast, lower-paying roles include Retail Data Analyst (-$38,069) and Financial Systems Analyst Oracle Cloud ERP (-$38,902), suggesting significant salary dispersion across job titles.

Geographic location shows limited explanatory power. North Dakota is the only state with a notable negative coefficient (-$16,340), while most states cluster closely together, indicating that state alone is not a strong differentiator of salary once other factors are controlled for.

Model Limitations

The RMSE of $35,616 and MAE of $26,797 indicate that prediction errors remain substantial. This reflects the inherent complexity of salary determination, which is influenced by factors not available in this dataset, such as individual years of experience, educational background, company size, and negotiation outcomes. Despite these limitations, the model provides directional insights into how job title, location, skills, and employment conditions influence compensation levels.
